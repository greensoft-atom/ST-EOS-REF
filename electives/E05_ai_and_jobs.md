# Elective E05 — AI and Jobs: Fear, Reality & New Skills

*Short elective · 60 minutes · All members · HR / leadership welcome*

**Slides:** [slides/E05_ai_and_jobs.md](slides/E05_ai_and_jobs.md)

---

## Objectives

1. Separate **automation**, **augmentation**, and **replacement** narratives  
2. Identify **skills that rise** in AI-assisted research & government work  
3. Personal plan for **collaboration with technical colleagues**  

---

## 1. Myths vs reality (12 min)

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| “Everyone replaced in 2 years” | Uneven by task; high-touch judgment persists |
| “No jobs in government” | Roles shift — corpus owners, verifiers, product |
| “Only programmers benefit” | Clear communicators who VERIFY gain leverage |
| “AI removes all drudgery” | New drudgery: checking AI, fixing corpus |

---

## 2. Task-level view (15 min)

| Task | AI role | Human role |
|------|---------|------------|
| First draft summary | Strong assist | Verify, approve |
| Grant eligibility **decision** | Info only | Decide |
| Inbox sorting | Assist | Handle exceptions |
| Relationship / negotiation | Weak | Lead |
| Ethical judgment | Weak | Lead |
| Corpus curation | Assist structure | Own accuracy |

**Framework:** Automate **drafts**; elevate **judgment**.

## 6. Organizational responsibility (5 min)

- Training time is **work time**  
- Review capacity must be **budgeted** ([08](08_trust_verification.md) T2)  
- No punitive metrics for “AI caught your error” — culture of improvement  

---

## 7. Takeaways

1. **Augmentation** beats replacement for most public-sector roles.  
2. **Judgment + verification** skills increase in value.  
3. **Corpus and eval** are new core work — not only IT.
