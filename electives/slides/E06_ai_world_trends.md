# Slide 03 | Regulatory Themes

## On screen

**Common policy themes (not legal advice)**

| Theme | Plain meaning |
|-------|---------------|
| **Risk-based approach** | Stricter rules for higher-impact uses |
| **Transparency** | Users know it's AI; sources where possible |
| **Data protection** | Classification, residency, consent |
| **Human oversight** | Meaningful review for important decisions |
| **Documentation** | Model cards, corpus registers, audit trails |

**Facilitator:** Cite your **jurisdiction's official guidance** — do not invent law on slides.

## Speaker notes

Themes recur across EU, APAC, Americas — wording differs. Emphasize facilitator adds local pointers verbally or in handout. Room may ask "are we compliant?" — defer to legal/compliance unless you're authorized. Themes align with curriculum design intentionally.

# Slide 07 | Enterprise vs Consumer AI Globally

## On screen

**Global shift toward **governed** assistants**

| Consumer pattern | Enterprise / public pattern |
|------------------|-------------------------------|
| General chatbot | Corpus-bound Q&A |
| Vendor terms opaque | Classification & audit |
| Viral feature race | Tier gates & approval |
| "Move fast" | "Move accountable" |

**This curriculum trains the **right** column.**

## Speaker notes

Contrast explains why internal platform beats shadow consumer tools — aligns E02 work policy. Global trend is not "more ChatGPT" alone but document-grounded copilots. Public sector lags consumer on UX but leads on oversight — feature, not bug.

# Slide 08 | Activity — Headline Triage

## On screen

**START ACTIVITY — ~12 minutes**

**Groups triage sample headlines — response strategy:**

| Headline type | Suggested response |
|---------------|-------------------|
| "AGI achieved" | **Skeptical** — check primary source |
| "Ban all AI" | **Overbroad** — risk-based nuance |
| "Local LLM for government" | **Aligns** with sovereignty / [05](../../05_local_llm.md) |
| "Chatbot wrong deadline" | **Process + corpus** issue — not "AI banned" |

**Facilitator:** Prepare 5 real or synthetic headline cards for table debate.

## Speaker notes

Cards make it fun — competitive triage optional. Debrief: wrong deadline is operational fix (corpus, T2), not reason to abandon program. AGI headlines need primary source discipline. Ban-all is policy overreach compared to tier model.
