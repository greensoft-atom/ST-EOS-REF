# Slide 02 | Myths vs Reality

## On screen

**Headlines vs what we see in government & research**

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "Everyone replaced in 2 years" | Uneven by **task**; high-touch judgment persists |
| "No jobs in government" | Roles **shift** — corpus owners, verifiers, product |
| "Only programmers benefit" | Clear communicators who **VERIFY** gain leverage |
| "AI removes all drudgery" | New drudgery: checking AI, fixing corpus, eval loops |

**Pattern:** Tasks change faster than job titles.

## Speaker notes

Read myths with humor where safe — room may have heard them this week. Reality column is evidence-based for public-sector pace: slow procurement, strong judgment roles, verification-heavy workflows. Ask who feels more augmentation or anxiety — validate both.

# Slide 03 | Task-Level Framework

## On screen

**Automate drafts · Elevate judgment**

| Task | AI role | Human role |
|------|---------|------------|
| First draft summary | Strong assist | Verify, approve |
| Grant eligibility **decision** | Info only | **Decide** |
| Inbox sorting | Assist | Handle exceptions |
| Relationship / negotiation | Weak | **Lead** |
| Ethical judgment | Weak | **Lead** |
| Corpus curation | Assist structure | **Own accuracy** |

> **Framework:** Automate **drafts**; elevate **judgment**.

## Speaker notes

Core framework for the elective — return to it in activity. Eligibility **decision** row prevents dangerous automation thinking. Relationship and ethics rows reassure senior staff. Corpus curation elevates domain experts — not only IT career path.

# Slide 06 | Augmentation vs Replacement Spectrum

## On screen

**Where public-sector tasks land**

```
Full replacement ◄────────────────────────► Human-only
     (rare)              augmentation zone              (T3)
                          ▲ most of your job
```

| Zone | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Augmentation** | Drafts, search, sorting, tagging |
| **Human-only (T3)** | Awards, legal commits, disciplinary decisions |
| **Replacement (rare)** | Narrow, low-stakes, heavily supervised automation |

**Org strategy:** Expand augmentation; **protect** human-only list.

## Speaker notes

Visual spectrum reduces binary thinking. Most roles sit in the middle decade-plus. Replacement exists for narrow T1 tasks with sampling — not for judgment roles. Leadership owns human-only list — link E04 fairness and Lecture 08 T3.

# Slide 08 | Organizational Responsibility

## On screen

**Leaders and HR — culture enables adoption**

| Responsibility | Why |
|----------------|-----|
| Training time is **work time** | Literacy is job skill, not hobby |
| **Review capacity** budgeted | T2 content needs approver hours |
| No punitive **"AI caught your error"** metrics | Culture of improvement, not shame |
| Clear **approved tools** | Reduces shadow IT risk |

Refs: [08_trust_verification.md](../../08_trust_verification.md) (T2 gates)

> Augmentation fails when verification is **expected but not resourced**.

## Speaker notes

Speak directly to managers — unfunded mandate problem is common. If approvers have no calendar time, T2 becomes rubber stamp. Psychological safety for reporting AI errors improves corpus quality. Tie to organizational duty, not individual heroics.

# Slide 09 | Career Narratives — Three Paths

## On screen

**Roles growing in AI-assisted public orgs**

| Path | Who | Example contribution |
|------|-----|----------------------|
| **Corpus & domain** | Program staff, librarians, legal | Own accurate sources |
| **Verification & product** | Comms, QA, senior admins | Approve public outputs |
| **Technical enablement** | Dev, ops, data | Secure, reliable stack |

**All three** needed — not only hiring more engineers.

## Speaker notes

Counter "only coders win" myth with three-path table. Corpus path validates existing expertise. Verification path is career growth for comms/policy. Technical path is enabler. Ask room which path they already partially occupy — builds identity, not fear.
